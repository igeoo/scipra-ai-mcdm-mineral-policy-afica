TITLE: Lonmin accused of prolonging platinum strike by hiding revenue

South African platinum producer Lonmin, which was crippled by a five-month strike this year, could have met workers’ wage demands, had it not moved revenue through a Bermudan subsidiary, a lobby group has said. The world’s third-biggest platinum miner has moved earnings from South Africa to low-tax Bermuda using a unit called Western Platinum since at least 2006, the Cape Town-based Alternative Information and Development Centre (AIDC) said in a 41-page report.
